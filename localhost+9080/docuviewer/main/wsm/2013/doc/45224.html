Main bearings
The main bearings are of the angular contact type with offset inner races so that the balls transmit loads from one groove to the other along straight lines at a certain angle to the bearing axis.
The angle-contact ball bearings are designed for bearing combined loading (radial-axial loads).
Bearings of this type can bear direct axial loads in one direction only. In fact, under the action of a radial load inside the bearing, an axial force is created that must be counterbalanced by an axial force acting in the opposite direction: that is why these bearings are generally fitted back to back in pairs.
To replace the bearings proceed as follows:
-
heat the crankcase in an oven to 100 °C;
-
remove the bearing using a drift and hammer;
-
install the new bearing (while the crankcase is still hot) keeping it perfectly square in its seat using a tubular drift that only bears on the outer ring of the bearing;
-
allow the parts to cool and check that the bearing is securely seated in the crankcase half.
Note
Install all bearings with their writing up, fully against the bush.
To fit the bearings proceed as described below.
To refit bearing (22), insert roller bearing (3) with the "writing" side up, fully against bush (2) and make sure groove (A) is visible.
Fit snap ring (4) in groove (A) by blocking roller bearing (3) in bush (2).
To fit bearing (22) on crankcase (1), heat the clutch-side crankcase half (1) at a temperature between 95 °C and 110 °C, until reaching an even temperature on the crankcase surface.
Immediately after having heated the crankcase, fit bearing (22) completely inside the crankcase inner side, with the "writing" side facing the crankcase half.
Position the reference mark (B), that indicates the bush slot, parallel to the threaded hole (C) to fit the locking dowel.
Apply specified threadlocker on the special dowel thread (5).
Screw special dowel (5) on crankcase half (1) by inserting the conical side (E) in slot (D) of the previously fitted bush (A).
Tighten special dowel (5) to a torque of 16 Nm (Min. 15 Nm - Max. 17 Nm).
Apply threadlocker on dowel thread (6).
Tighten dowel (6) on crankcase half (1) and tighten it to 22 Nm (Min. 20 Nm - Max. 24 Nm).
After tightening, remove any excessive threadlocker.
To pre-tighten bearing (23), insert roller bearing (24) with the "writing" side down, fully against bush (25) and make sure groove (F) is visible.
Fit snap ring (26) in groove (F) by blocking roller bearing (24) in bush (25).
To fit bearing (23) on crankcase half (9), heat the chain-side crankcase half (9) at a temperature between 95 °C and 110 °C, until reaching an even temperature on the crankcase surface.
Immediately after having heated the crankcase half, fit bearing (23) fully against crankcase half outer side, with the "writing" side facing the crankcase half, making sure that groove (E) is completely visible.
From the crankcase half inner side, fit snap ring (27) in groove (E) by blocking bearing (23) in crankcase half (9).
Attention
On worn engines, the bearing holder bushes may no longer be tight fitted in the crankcases.
After having removed the bushes, check that the interference fit between crankcase and bush, with bearing fitted, is (0.105÷0.029) mm on the flywheel side and (0.126÷0.037) mm on the clutch side.
