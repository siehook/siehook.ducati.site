Removing the clutch slave cylinder
Warning
The clutch slave cylinder manufacturer advises against servicing the internal components of clutch slave cylinder due to the safety critical nature of this component.
Incorrect overhaul can endanger rider and passenger safety.
The replacement operations shall be limited to the following components: clutch slave cylinder, bleed valve, seals and complete clutch pushrod assembly.
Drain the clutch system (Draining the clutch hydraulic circuit).
Remove ties (A) and separate clutch line (2) from side stand cable (B).
Loosen screws (3) and take out clutch slave cylinder (1) keeping the O-ring (4) located inside it.
Remove dust cap (5) and bleed valve (6), and undo screw (7), taking care not to damage sealing washers (8): unit (1) has been released from hose (2).
Push the piston to force out all the fluid from inside the cap.
At this point, it is possible to slide also the clutch pushrod (9). Check the two O-rings (10) for wear and, if necessary, replace them.
If the two O-rings (10) are renewed, also change O-ring (4) of unit (1).
