Checking brake pad wear and replacing rear brake pads
Check through the slot between the two half callipers that there is at least 1 mm of friction material on the pads.
Important
If either of the pads is worn, both pads must be renewed as a set.
Change the brake pads as follows.
Remove the snap ring (8) from the pad retaining pin (9).
The split pin is on calliper inner side. Withdraw the brake pad retaining pin (9) and pull it out.
Remove the clip (10) from between the two calliper halves.
Remove the rear brake calliper by unscrewing the screws (11).
Force the brake pads apart to push the calliper pistons into their seats.
Remove the worn pads (12).
Fit the new pads.
Refit rear brake calliper stating and tightening the screws (11) to 25 Nm ±5%.
Insert pad retaining clip (10) and centring pin (9), locking it in place with ring (8).
Operate the brake pedal repeatedly so that the pads are bedded in against the disc by the force of the brake fluid.
Check that the brake fluid level in the tank is between the MIN and MAX marks. If this is not the case, unscrew the tank cap (6) and top it up.
Being the brake callipers a safety component of the engine, follow instructions indicated in "Removing the front brake system", "Removing the rear brake control", and, during the refitting, tighten rear brake calliper screws (11) to 25 Nm ±5%.
