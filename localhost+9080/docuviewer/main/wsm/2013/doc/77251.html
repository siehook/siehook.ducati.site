Spark plugs replacement
Check the colour of the ceramic insulation around the central electrode: an even, light brown colour indicates the engine is in good condition and running at the right temperature.
Inspect the centre electrode for wear and check spark plug gap, which should be: 0.8-0.1 mm.
Important
Check the gap between the central and side electrodes. Replace the spark plug if taken value is different from the specified one or the spark plug shows clear signs of carbon settling.
Warning
Do not use spark plugs with inadequate thermal rating or incorrect thread length.
The spark plug must be securely installed.
If a spark plug is loose, it can overheat and damage the engine.
Spark plug type
Make: NGK
Type: MAR10A-J
Remove both seats (Removing the seat).
Remove the left and right-hand side instrument panel (Removing the panel).
Remove the front half-fairing (Removing the front half-fairing).
Remove the tank fairings (Removing the tank fairings).
Reach the vertical head spark plugs by lifting the tank and loosening screws (1) and (2) and installing a buffer below it.
Disconnect fuel sensor connector (3), fuel pump connector (4) and the quick-release fittings (5) from the flange.
To reach the horizontal head spark plugs loosen radiator screws (6) and move radiator slightly up.
Slide out spark plug caps (7) and use tool part no. 88713.2877 to replace spark plugs (8) and tighten them to 12 Nm (Min. 11 Nm - Max. 13 Nm).
Place caps (7) in place and tighten relevant nuts.
Work in the same way on both heads.
Fasten radiator to support, start and tighten screws (6) to a torque of 10 Nm ±10%.
Connect fuel sensor connector (3), fuel pump connector (4) and the quick-release fittings (5) from the flange.
Refit tank and tighten the screws (1) and (2) to a torque of 10 Nm ±10%.
Refit the tank fairings (Refitting the tank fairings).
Refit the front half-fairing (Refitting the front half-fairing).
Refit the left and right-hand side instrument panel (Refitting the panel).
Refit both seats (Refitting the seat).
