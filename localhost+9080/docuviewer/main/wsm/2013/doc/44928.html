Note
For clarity, the figures show the engine removed from the frame.
Loosen and remove screws (1), (2) and (16) that fix water pump cover (3) to generator cover (4) and recover the relevant washer.
Remove the water pump cover (3) and recover dowels (5).
Clean the pump housing from any scale. Check the bearing wear by turning the impeller shaft (6); in case of excessive clearance, it is necessary to replace them as follows.
Remove the snap ring (7) from the impeller shaft, and the washer (8) underneath.
Slide off the impeller (6) with the sealing ring (9) from the outside.
Recover ceramic washer and seal that form the counter-washer (14).
Undo and remove the screws (10) with washer (11).
Working from the impeller side, use a suitable drift to press on the inner ring of end bearing (12) until it can be extracted from the cover.
Use the same technique to remove the other bearing (13).
Remove the inner spacer (15).
Check the conditions of the mechanic seal (9) and the counter-washer (14): they must not feature any deformation, crack or excessive wear. In the case of damage, both components must be replaced.
