Adjusting the position of the gear change and rear brake pedals
The position of the gear change and rear brake pedals in relation to the footpegs can be adjusted to suit the preferred riding position.
To adjust the position of the gearchange pedal, proceed as follows:
Use an open ended wrench to hold the fixed joint (1) on the flat (A) and loosen the lock nut (2).
Turn the screw (4) to detach the mobile joint (3) from the gear change lever.
Turn the mobile joint (3), until the gear change pedal is set to the desired position.
Connect gear change lever and mobile joint (3) by tightening screw (4) to 10 Nm ±10%.
Tighten the lock nut (2) onto the fixed joint (1).
To adjust the position of the rear brake pedal, proceed as follows.
Loosen check nut (5).
Turn pedal travel adjusting screw (6) until pedal is in the desired position.
Tighten the lock nut (5) to 5 Nm ±10%.
Verify, by acting manually on the pedal, that there is a play of about 1.5 ÷2 mm before starting the braking operation.
If not, modify the length of the cylinder push-rod in the following mode.
Slacken off the check nut (7) on the cylinder pushrod.
Screw the rod into the fork (8) to increase play, or unscrew it to reduce play.
Tighten the check nut (7) to 2.5 Nm ±10% and check play again.
